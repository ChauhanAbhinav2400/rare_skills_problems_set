
=======================================================================================How to quickly tell if a binary number is divisible by a fixed power of 2
=======================================================================================

Description
A binary number is divisible by 2ⁿ if and only if the last n bits are all 0.

This works because 2ⁿ in binary is 1 followed by n zeros. For a number to be divisible by 2ⁿ, it must be a multiple of 2ⁿ, which means all bits representing values less than 2ⁿ must be zero.

Divisibility rules:

Divisible by 2¹ (2): Last 1 bit is 0
Divisible by 2² (4): Last 2 bits are 00
Divisible by 2³ (8): Last 3 bits are 000
Divisible by 2⁴ (16): Last 4 bits are 0000
Examples:

10100₂ ends in 00 → divisible by 4, not by 8
11000₂ ends in 000 → divisible by 8 (and also by 4 and 2)
10010₂ ends in 10 → divisible by 2, not by 4
10001₂ ends in 1 → not divisible by 2
Example:
What is the highest power of 2 that 110100₂ is divisible by?

Answer:
Last 2 bits: 00 → divisible by 4
Last 3 bits: 100 → not divisible by 8
The highest power of 2 it's divisible by is 4.
