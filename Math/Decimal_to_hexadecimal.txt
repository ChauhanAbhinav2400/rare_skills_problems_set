=======================================================================================
Converting Decimal to Hexadecimal
=======================================================================================
Description
Decimal is base 10. You know how to convert decimal to binary: divide by 2, track remainders, then read backwards.

Converting decimal to hexadecimal works the SAME way, but with base 16:

Repeatedly divide by 16.
Each remainder is one hex digit.
Stop when the quotient is 0.
Read the remainders from last to first.
Digits above 9 are written with letters:
10=A, 11=B, 12=C, 13=D, 14=E, 15=F

Examples:

47 ÷ 16 = 2 remainder 15 → hex digits 2, F → 0x2F

172 ÷ 16 = 10 remainder 12 → hex digits A, C → 0xAC

255 ÷ 16 = 15 remainder 15 → hex digits F, F → 0xFF

2895 ÷ 16 = 181 remainder 15 → digit F
181 ÷ 16 = 11 remainder 5 → digits B, 4
So reading backwards: B 4 F → 0xB4F
(this is a three-digit hexadecimal number)

48879 ÷ 16 = 3054 remainder 15 → digit F
3054 ÷ 16 = 190 remainder 14 → digit E
190 ÷ 16 = 11 remainder 14 → digit E
11 ÷ 16 = 0 remainder 11 → digit B
So reading backwards: B E E F → 0xBEEF
(this is a four-digit hexadecimal number)

Just like binary, leading zeros don’t change the value:
0x00AC = 0xAC.

Question 1
What is the hexadecimal value of 0?
answer-0x0



Question 2
What is the hexadecimal value of 10?
answer-0xA




Question 3
What is the hexadecimal value of 15?
answer-0xf
