====================================================================================
Fixed-point multiplication rounding up
====================================================================================
Description
Fixed-point Multiplication Rounding Up
When we multiply two fixed-point numbers together, the result is rounded down if the product does not perfectly divide the scaling factor.

For example, if our fixed-point numbers have a scaling factor of 100, then if we multiply 1.66 * 1.33 we get 2.2087. However, the final result will be 2.20 because we only store two decimals.

Thus, the product was rounded down.

When to Round Up
As noted in the lesson on division rounding up, it is sometimes desirable to slightly overestimate quantities rather than underestimate them. For example, we want to slightly overestimate the amount a borrower owes to a protocol so the protocol doesn't lose money.

The Formula
Thus, if we want to multiply two fixed-point numbers together and round the result up, we use the following method:

divUp(storedValue_x * storedValue_y, scalingFactor);

Examples
Example 1: 1.66 and 1.33 are fixed-point numbers with a scaling factor of 100. What is the represented value of their product, rounded up?

Answer: 2.21

The stored values of x and y are 166 and 133, respectively. Their product is 22078. When we divide the product by 100, we get 220 with a remainder of 78. Since the remainder is non-zero, we add 1 to the result and get 221. We then divide the stored value by the scaling factor to get the represented value of 2.21

Example 2: 0.01 and 0.05 are fixed-point numbers with scaling factor of 100. What is the represented value of their product, rounded up?

Answer: 0.01

The stored values are 1 and 5, and the product is 5. When we divide by 100, we get 0, but rounded up, we get 1. When scaled, the represented value is 0.01.

Example 3: 0.00 and 0.05 are fixed-point numbers with a scaling factor of 100. What is the represented value of their product, rounded up?

Answer: 0

0 / 100 has a remainder of zero, so we do not round it up.

Example 4: 10.0 and 20.0 are fixed-point numbers with scaling factor of 100. What is the represented value of their product, rounded up?

Answer: 200

10 * 20 = 200. 200 is divisible by 100 (remainder is 0), so we do not round the result up.
